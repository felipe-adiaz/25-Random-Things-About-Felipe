25 random things about Felipe

This website was thoughtfully developed and coded by me to offer a deeper insight into the 25 things mentioned in my essay. I sincerely appreciate you taking the time to explore more about my story. I’ve taken the liberty of expanding on the details and providing further context around the events illustrated by the images and videos, in their descriptions.

Visiting Ambler Stadium

My inner child was absolutely thrilled to visit those courts. Duke was a dream school for me as a junior player hoping to play college tennis in the U.S., partly because it was home to Brazilian junior players like Bruno Semenzato and Henrique Cunha, who was ranked among the top 5 junior players in the world at the time. Cunha made history playing for Duke in the NCAA, winning the All-America title three times.

A Full 'Cameron Crazie' Experience

As a huge Coach K fan, attending a basketball game was an unforgettable experience. My namesake and new Brazilian friend, Luis Felipe '26, had a spare ticket for me. I spent an amazing evening with new friends—Pedro '19, Lais '26, Fabi, and Camilla '26—appearing on the stadium screen, enjoying dinner, and hitting Tavern Tuesday later. Can’t wait to camp out for season tickets!

Practicing in Argentina

This was published in a local journal prior to my trip to Tandil: "Young tennis players from Brasília aim to improve by following the example of Argentine players on the global circuit." We had the opportunity to practice with former coaches of some of the best Argentine professionals of all time, including Juan Martín del Potro, former US Open champion; Juan Mónaco, a former top 10 player in the world; and Mariano Zabaleta, a former top 20 player. We spent a couple of weeks practicing at the same club where they trained as kids, hosted by Cristina at her pension, who cooked the best meals.

Tennis Practice Session

Even though I had to stop competing around the age of 15, after a few years' break, I was able to play again, as much as my body allowed. I could compete a little and even play for the Derby University team while living in England. Tennis has always been a part of my life, and to this day, I play whenever I can, though sometimes my body charges me a high price if I push it too hard.

My First Passion: The Guitar

I started playing the guitar when I was 8 years old. I used to visit my grandmother and play with all of my uncle's instruments from a very young age. By the time I started playing songs on my little sister's keyboard, my mom figured it was time to give me my first guitar. Blues is definitely one of the genres that most represents my musical inclinations, especially when playing the guitar.
